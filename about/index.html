About Me

An extremely subjective creative personality type quiz once described me as "always the perpetual student" (shout-out to Adobe). I love learning new things, which might explain why I spent 22 years of my life in school, why I turned my photography hobby into a sporadic freelance operation, or why I decided to try coding this website myself.

Let's start with the STEM side of this portfolio. I hold a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering from Boston University, where I worked on nanoparticle drug delivery and the fluid mechanics of swellable structures. For a few years afterward, I worked as a researcher at Stanford University, where I investigated stem cell health and used CRISPR to study cancer-related genomics. Recently, I completed my M.S. and Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at UC Berkeley in the Embodied Dexterity Group, where I was an NSF Graduate Research Fellow developing robotic systems to augment human hand function in people with spinal cord injuries. Now, I'm a Research Engineer at Gridware, working to protect and monitor our fragile electrical grid.

The other half of my portfolio encompasses my second passion: sports. I've competed in sports for as long as I can remember, and while I'm admittedly a bit washed-up now, sports photography has allowed me to remain involved in sports at the highest levels. Soccer will always be my one true love and I've had the great honor of creating images for the U.S. Soccer Federation, Bay FC, the San Jose Earthquakes, and a number of Division I Athletic programs.

Please reach out to me here if you'd like to connect; I love a good conversation on science, robots, soccer, or anything in between!
