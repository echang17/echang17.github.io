About Me

Always hungry. Always foolish.

Hi, I’m Erin. I’m a scientist, an engineer, an insatiable pit of continuous hunger, a washed-up soccer player, and a sports photographer.

I graduated from Boston University with a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering, where I worked on nanoparticle drug delivery and the fluid mechanics of swellable structures. When I wasn’t slaving away at problem sets or in the lab, I could usually be found on the indoor soccer courts or eating my way around Boston.

Now, I conduct research at Stanford University, where I grow stem cells and use CRISPR to manipulate chromatin architecture. I am also an in-game and studio photographer for Stanford Athletics, where I cover a variety of sports including Football, Volleyball, Soccer, Basketball, and Water Polo. Outside of Stanford, I have been contracted for the U.S. Women's National Soccer Team, the San Jose Earthquakes, and Duke University Athletics.
