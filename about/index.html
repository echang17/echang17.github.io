About Me

Always hungry. Always foolish.

Hi, I’m Erin. I’m a scientist, an engineer, an insatiable pit of continuous hunger (for both food and knowledge), a washed-up soccer player, and a sports photographer.

I graduated from Boston University with a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering, where I worked on nanoparticle drug delivery and the fluid mechanics of swellable structures. I love building new things, so I decided to host this website on Github to tinker with web design and some basic coding!

Now, I conduct research at Stanford University, where I work with stem cells and use CRISPR to manipulate chromatin architecture. I am also an in-game and studio photographer for Stanford Athletics, where I cover a variety of sports including Football, Volleyball, Soccer, Basketball, and Water Polo. Outside of Stanford Athletics, I have created images for the U.S. Women's National Soccer Team, the San Jose Earthquakes, Duke University Athletics, USC Athletics, Street Soccer USA, and F45 Training.
