About Me

Always hungry. Always foolish.

Hi, I’m Erin. I’m a scientist, an engineer, an insatiable pit of continuous hunger (for both food and knowledge), a washed-up soccer player, and a sports photographer.

I graduated from Boston University with a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering, where I worked on nanoparticle drug delivery and the fluid mechanics of swellable structures. I love building new things, so I decided to host this website on Github to tinker with web design and some basic coding!

After BU, I conducted research at Stanford University, where I worked with stem cells and used CRISPR to manipulate chromatin architecture. I am currently a Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. student and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at UC Berkeley, where I work on robotic systems to augment human dexterity in the Embodied Dexterity Group.

In my free time, I am also a freelance sports photographer. I work primarily with Stanford Athletics, US Soccer, and the San Jose Earthquakes, but have created images for many other Division I athletics programs, as well as Street Soccer USA and F45 Training.
